
# Table Rules

Welcome! Before discussing setting, or game rules, or other matters, we need to discuss behavioral expectations at the table. I’m going to make this brief.

## I record, DO Not Share

## Hard Topics

- **No Sexual Violence at the Table:** Any other presentation of this topic should be minimal or ambiguous: for example, a character could have been abused without explicitly addressing the topic.
	- The furthest I'd go is an unhappy arranged marriage.
	- The intent is what matters here (I.E. a character who is unsure about what another PC/NPC wants because they crit failed a roll does not apply, because that's hilarious)
- **Consensual Sex/Romance:** I usually follow the policy of fade to black, a euphemistic description, or describing the aftermath.
- **Other Hard Topics - Please inform the GM:** This setting includes ~~slavery (ranging from ‘decent’ conditions to incredibly cruel)~~, violence, xenophobia, sexism, (fantasy) racism, low standards of living, an honestly horrifying afterlife, and children dying. If you have a problem with these subjects (or any others), please bring it up with your GM. Your GM should work to minimize the prevalence of these topics in your game session.
	- I might have a survey to fill out next time.

## Other Rules
- **Respect the Setting - Memelord vs Eccentric:** Players should respect the world. That being said, player characters are wildcards who are inherently _not normal_, the world includes magic (some of which is silly), and this setting isn’t 100% grimdark either. There’s a line between _I am a memelord lol_ and a character who is _eccentric_.
- **Respect the Player Characters - Assume Competency:** GMs should assume the PCs are _somewhat_ competent, unless they have hinderances dictating otherwise. This means any decisions that are likely being made due to a lack of information/misunderstanding on the player’s part should be given a warning. For example, PCs returning from adventure who want to appear in a court might realize that they are utterly filthy should likely be warned that this wouldn’t be favored by the royalty of the realm.
- **Keep OOC chatter in Line:** You should not be having side conversations in game. Try not to joke about literally *everything* that happens at the table in an OOC way. If you can make an IC joke, its usually funnier. This is more of a magnitude thing than an existence thing.
- **Arguing Rules:** You are allowed to briefly argue for your perspective on a rule. Powergamers beware: if it works that way for the PC it also works that way for the NPCs. If you can peasant railgun, so can your foes.
- **Serious GM Errors:** If the GM makes a serious error that results in a poor outcome for your character, please discuss it after the game/during break if possible. The GM, if they choose, can make this up to your later in game. Retconning is difficult for many reasons.
- **Talking in front of NPCs:** Discussing plans while in front of an NPC can be taken by the GM as whispering or talking in front of that NPC in character. There are powers (mind link) that allow mental communication. This does not include questions about the setting directed at the GM. This rule should be treated with respect to the assume minimal competency rule.
- **Fast Turns:** Players should declare their turns as quickly as possible. Sometimes the simple option really is the best one. Indecision on the player’s part could represent indecision on the character’s part - you are allowed to hold your action. GMs should be more lenient with newer players.
- **Food is not on the GM:** This is a pretty classic rule at most tables. Thank you.
	- Also, not a picky eater.
