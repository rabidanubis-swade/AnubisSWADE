# Welcome to Sanguia
In many ways, Sanguia represents a typical fantasy kitchen sink setting - it has elves, dwarves, humans, gods, dragons, and monsters. There are however a few central concepts that differentiate it from a ‘typical’ fantasy setting. 

Firstly, the Cultures of Sanguia are more important than the various races present there. Races modify a culture rather than being it - for example, a half-orc would likely exhibit the more rough aspects of their culture, but how that roughness would be displayed would differ for an Avaronian and Zephyral half-orc. Long-living elves would likely exhibit haughty attitudes whether they are Deltan or Svedtalan.

Additionally, the Cosmology of the setting uses Ancient Mediterranean Mythologies (Egyptian, Sumerian, and Greek) as a base, then draws from other mythologies for flavor. Here are a few examples of these impacts:
- The Afterlife rewards greatness rather than goodness, and those buried with expensive goods can get better deals.
- The Greater Deities (Gods) are not divided into clearly ‘good’ and ‘evil’ categories.
- Months are defined by a Highstorm (think category 6 magic hurricane) that occurs every seven weeks. This storm dictates a great deal of weather on Sanguia. Years consist of seven months, with each Greater Deity (God) claiming their own.
- Most sapient creatures on Sanguia (such as humans, elves, ect.) are composed of three main parts: their Khet (Elemental Body), their Ka (Spirit), and their Ba (Soul). The Ren (Name) of a creature ties these aspects together.

# Table Rules

Welcome! Before discussing setting, or game rules, or other matters, we need to discuss behavioral expectations at the table. I’m going to make this brief.

#### I record, I DO Not Share
- I use this to help with my in game note taking.

#### Hard Topics
- **No Sexual Violence at the Table:** Any other presentation of this topic should be minimal or ambiguous: for example, a character could have been abused without explicitly addressing the topic.
	- The furthest I'd go is an unhappy arranged marriage.
	- The intent is what matters here (I.E. a character who is unsure about what another PC/NPC wants because they crit failed a roll does not apply, because that's hilarious)
- **Consensual Sex/Romance:** I usually follow the policy of fade to black, a euphemistic description, or describing the aftermath.
- **Other Hard Topics - Please inform the GM:** This setting includes slavery (ranging from ‘decent’ conditions to incredibly cruel), violence, xenophobia, sexism, (fantasy) racism, low standards of living, an honestly horrifying afterlife, and children dying. If you have a problem with these subjects (or any others), please bring it up with your GM. Your GM should work to minimize the prevalence of these topics in your game session.
	- I might have a survey to fill out next time.

#### Appropriate Characters
- **Respect the Setting - Memelord vs Eccentric:** Players should respect the world. That being said, player characters are wildcards who are inherently _not normal_, the world includes magic (some of which is silly), and this setting isn’t 100% grimdark either. There’s a line between _I am a memelord lol_ and a character who is _eccentric_.
- **Respect the Player Characters - Assume Competency:** GMs should assume the PCs are _somewhat_ competent, unless they have hinderances dictating otherwise. This means any decisions that are likely being made due to a lack of information/misunderstanding on the player’s part should be given a warning. 
	- For example, PCs returning from adventure who want to appear in a court might realize that they are utterly filthy should likely be warned that this wouldn’t be favored by the royalty of the realm.

#### Other Behavior at the Table
- **Keep OOC chatter in Line:** You should not be having side conversations in game. Try not to joke about literally *everything* that happens at the table in an OOC way. 
	- If you can make an IC joke, its usually funnier. 
	- This is more of a magnitude thing than an existence thing.
- **Arguing Rules:** You are allowed to briefly argue for your perspective on a rule. Powergamers beware: if it works that way for the PC it also works that way for the NPCs. If you can peasant railgun, so can your foes.
- **Serious GM Errors:** If the GM makes a serious error that results in a poor outcome for your character, please discuss it after the game/during break if possible. The GM, if they choose, can make this up to your later in game.
- **Talking in front of NPCs:** Discussing plans while in front of an NPC can be taken by the GM as whispering or talking in front of that NPC in character. There are powers (mind link) that allow mental communication. This does not include questions about the setting directed at the GM. 
	- This rule should be treated with respect to the assume minimal competency rule.
- **Fast Turns:** Players should declare their turns as quickly as possible. Sometimes the simple option really is the best one. Indecision on the player’s part could represent indecision on the character’s part - you are allowed to hold your action. 
	- GMs should be more lenient with newer players.
- **Plz Bring GM Snack:** :D

# House Rules
This setting makes use of rules from both Savage Worlds Adventure Edition (Henceforth SWADE) and SWADE Fantasy Companion (Henceforth FC). If there's a feature in both, always use the Fantasy Companion version.

#### Setting Rules
- Dynamic Backlash (Only on Reality Bending Spells, such as Plane Shift, Teleportation, Wish, Resurrection, ect.) (SWADE 138)
- Wound Cap (SWADE 141)

#### House Rules
- GM bennies cannot be spent on Wildcards (Wildcards can still spend their own bennies)

# Character Creation
The character creation steps for Savage Worlds in this setting consists of the following steps (complete in order).

> [!info] Where to Find Stuff
> The following should come from the Fantasy Companion:
> - Ancestry/Race
> - Gear
> - Powers
> 
>Here’s some things that will likely come from SWADE:
> - Many Edges and Hinderances, esp. background edges
>
>Things that will come from both:
> - Edges and Hinderances

#### Step 1: Select a Culture
Read the introduction to each culture and select one from those listed below. Questions are included in these sections to help flesh out your character. **Make sure to take the languages listed in "Summary". If only one language is listed, you get another of your choice.**

There's no need to read past "Extra Details" unless you're curious about a certain aspect of that culture and wish to expand on it.

> [!info] Bonus Skill Points
> Note that taking edges and hindrances that align with your culture will give you bonus skill points to spend later on.

> [!question] But What if I want to Know More?
> You've read the stuff, you have something interesting: unfortunately, you just got a summary. You want to embrace a certain god, or religion, or the inner workings of some organization.
> No need to fear, just ask your GM for additional details.

#### Step 2: Select a Race
Select an Ancestry from the Fantasy Companion. Additional races, or races with significant modifications or departures from traditional fantasy canon are listed below.

> [!warning] Outsider
> If your race has Outsider as a hinderance, discuss with the GM. The hinderance may need to be replaced with another Hinderance if the campaign is not taking place in an area where the outsider hinderance would be relevant (such as playing a half-elf in a campaign focused on The Delta).

#### Step 3: Select Hinderances
Select additional hinderances: two major, four minor, or a major and two minor hinderances.

> [!warning] Hindrances from Other Sources
> These hinderances must be added separately from those gained from edges, arcane backgrounds, or any other source. For example, if you select an ancestry that has Mean (Minor) as a racial hinderance, it does not count towards the totals listed here.

#### Step 4: Select Edges
Take any two edges you desire - make sure to take notes of requirements for these edges, because you must fulfill them by the end of character creation.

> [!warning] Edges from Other Sources
> These edges are separate from those granted must be added separately from those gained from edges, arcane backgrounds, or any other source. For example, if you choose a race that has the Attractive edge included, you still get two other edges.

> [!warning] Arcane Background
> Make sure to pick powers exclusively from the Fantasy Companion

#### Step 5: Assign Attributes
Attributes start at d4. You have 5 points to distribute among them. Each step costs 1 point. **Attributes may not typically be raised beyond d12.** Make sure attributes fulfill the requirements for selected edges. 

#### Step 6: Assign Skills

##### Default Skills
Athletics, Common Knowledge, Notice, Persuasion, and Stealth are core skills and start at d4 for free, unless something from before states otherwise.

##### Skill Points
Now, assign 12 points for skills. Each step costs 1 point up to and equal to its linked attribute; then 2 points per die type higher than its linked attribute. Skills may not typically be raised beyond d12. **Make sure skills fulfill the requirements for selected edges.**

> For example, with a d6 Agility it costs 1 point to gain a d4 in fighting, 1 point (2 total) to raise it from a d4 to a d6, and 2 points (4 total) to raise it from a d6 to a d8.

> [!warning]
> If your character does not have the campaign language, you can spend two skill points to gain the Linguist edge. (Even if Smarts is a d4)

> [!check] Bonus Skill Points
> Add one bonus skill point for each of the following that is true at this point, and add them to your 12 point pool.
> - You have at least one hindrance with the mechanical tag, and at least one without it (these may come from any source)
> - You have at least one edge OR hindrance that is commonly associated with your culture (these may come form any source) 
> - You answered all the background questions suggested in the culture background, and included them in a description of a backstory you sent to the GM

##### Agility Skills
- Athletics
- Boating (Piloting Airships is tied in with Boating via the Renaissance Man edge)
- Fighting
- Riding
- Shooting
- Stealth
- Thievery

##### Smarts Skills
- Academics (Research, Science, and Academics are combined)
- Battle
- Common Knowledge
- Gambling
- Handyman (Note: Replaces Repair)
- Healing
- Notice
- Occult
- Survival
- Taunt

##### Spirit Skills
- Intimidation
- Performance
- Persuasion

##### Arcane Skills  
Your arcane skill is provided by your Arcane Background. Make sure when you take powers that they come from the Fantasy Companion.

#### Step 7: Starting Gear and Lifestyle
You start with 300 gold. All gear purchased should come from Fantasy Companion (chapter starts on page 47).

#### Step 8: Derived Stats
- Standard Pace is 6 and may be adjusted by ancestral abilities, Edges, or Hindrances.
- Parry is 2 plus half of Fighting. Shields and certain weapons may provide a bonus to parry.
- Toughness is 2 plus half of Vigor, plus any Armor. Track the amount of armor in parentheses like this—Toughness: 11 (2). This means 2 points of the total 11 Toughness comes from armor, and the other 9 come from vigor and other modifiers.
    Armor. An Armor Piercing (AP) attack could bypass those 2 points but not the other 9.
