###### Summary
To the Temple of the Twin Goddesses, love and life are two sides of the same coin, and one leads to another. A world of love is a world that nurtures. The temple praises [[Deities#Ianna, Goddess of Love and War|The Lioness]] and [[Deities#Nilvantra, Goddess of Life and Knowledge|Cultivation]] as two sides of the same coin, and revere the god-king [[Deities#Sarru Zalalahara|Sarru Zalalahara]] as their husband. A series of divinely appointed priestesses and their servants run the temple.

While it is the State Religion in the Delta and plays a major role in the life of [[Deltan]] individuals, the Temple of the Twin Goddesses has locations open across the globe where they conduct research on a variety of biomes, sponsor academic conferences, provide marriage counseling, test illegal substances, and sometimes perform other acts of love in exchange for a donation.

These temples are operated by divinely appointed [[Priestess (TotTG)|Priestesses]] and the servants that care for them. The most popular festival they host is the [[Fertility Festival]] across Sanguia.

###### Symbol
The head of a lioness with a 'mane' formed of vines.

# In the Delta
The Temple of the Twin Goddesses, as the state religion, is able to pass laws, officiates all weddings, judge certain crimes, and educates it citizens (or decides who is allowed to). They operate in a separate apparatus from the nobility, where neither technically outranks the other.

# Outside of The Delta
Many individuals outside The Delta still interact with the Temple. Women who leave civilization to seek a life abroad (known as the [[Balu]]) frequently still believe in their home faith. Others donate to the temple, such as Falcotosian merchants, in an effort to curry its favor. The temple will sometimes interview individuals to get an understanding - and properly document - the rise and fall of barbarian civilizations.

While the Temple's messages are centered on [[Deltan]] customs and values, the Temple isn't afraid to change their messaging slightly to appeal to foreign audiences. Talking to the mighty [[Zephyral]], they emphasize the might of Ianna.

#### Seba-dra Sects
While it is normal for temples to gear themselves to the services required by the local populace, Seba-dra Sects go beyond that, either blending with local faiths or diverging so thoroughly from traditional cannon that in practice they operate very differently. The temples of these sects officially remain part of the Temple of the Twin Goddesses, however. Seba-dra Sects are looked upon by the main temple with a combination of admiration and distaste.
> [!translation] Seba-dra is Sumerian for (Grain + Split)

#### Heresies
Recently, the [[Temple of Nephtys]] sprung up with backing from the [[Queen of the Storm]], acting as the only major operating heresy of the Temple of the Twin Goddess (meaning that they have Priestesses who are)

# Temple Canon


## View of [[Deities#Ianna, Goddess of Love and War|Ianna]]
They worship the Lioness as a leader of men, a lover of all, a passionate soul, and the most beautiful woman to ever grace Sanguia. Sometimes, Ianna is depicted as being irrationally passionate - the Temple of the Twin Goddesses views this as a forgivable (or even admirable) flaw, especially among women. They also view women who give to forms of direct violence to be trying to ‘upstage’ the goddess (though they skim over this message when dealing with 'barbarians').

### View of [[Deities#Nilvantra, Goddess of Life and Knowledge|Nilvantra]]
They worship her sister goddess, Cultivation, as emphasizing growth and wisdom. They celebrate academics, and encourage educators and education across cultures.

## View of other [[Deities#Lesser Deities|Lesser Deities]]
- [[Deities#Sarru Zalalahara]] is revered, although not directly worshipped. They understand that when he dies he will go to take over the [[Field of Reeds]] in the [[Underworld]].
- [[Satet, the Guiding River]] is viewed positively as the daughter of both goddesses and [[Deities#Sarru Zalalahara]] and as an inspiring voice.

# [[Priestess (TotTG)|Priestesses]]
Priestesses of the Temple of the Twin Goddesses always come from and are chosen in The Delta, typically by a sign from the goddesses; these signs can be varied and range from the altar lighting up to another priestess getting a vision. They enter training soon after they are selected, becoming a [[Priestess (TotTG)#Young Priestess|Young Priestess]]. Priestesses are exclusively female, with the exception of [[Nephtys]].

Temples, no matter how small, are operated by a pair of them. Usually one representing aspects of [[Deities#Ianna, Goddess of Love and War|Ianna]] and another representing aspects of [[Deities#Nilvantra, Goddess of Life and Knowledge|Nilvantra]].
