In their early adolescence, the daughters of well off merchants and nobility frequently seek a higher-ranking noble girl or woman to offer their services to until they come of age or are married (typically until 16-21 years old). In exchange, Emedu are given connections, presentation at a more prestigious court, and taught the ways of courtly living.

Emedu are typically given a thin collar (like a choker) to symbolize their status as a servant. Although the relationship is technically slavery, it would be better to imagine it as a combination of a lady-in-waiting, a handmaiden, an oath of fealty, an apprenticeship, and a sorority. Emedu are typically served by other slaves, with the Emedu often coordinating them for their mistresses. Realistically, an Emedu could choose to leave the service of their mistress though this is considered very shameful.

> [!note]
> A modern observer may reasonably conclude that the aesthetic of slavery, even if entirely symbolic, is sexist and reinforces Deltan sexism by showing that a even a noble girl should know how to serve. This is an especially reasonable conclusion given the lack of a similar relationship for noble men.

### Circles
It is typical for girls to be brought in as ‘circles’ of three or four individuals of similar age. A high-ranking mistress typically maintains an older, experienced circle of Emedu and a younger circle that has less experience. Priestess (ToTG) are typically assigned to focus on the education of a circle. 

Circles are very cliquish, with circles from the same mistress (or family) acting as something of a sorority. The girls in a circle spend a great deal of time with each other, with many living in the house of their owner, and practice similar traditions (see: Mild Hazing).

#### Interactions Between Circles
Circles frequently interact with each other at sleepovers, parties, and other events. The rank of a Emedu is determined by the rank of her mistress rather than her own rank - meaning the daughter of a merchant serving a [[Deltan#Sarrim/Sarrimisa (Relatives of a Sarru or a Lugal)|Sarrimisa]] could boss around the daughter of a lord.

### Younger Mistresses
The Emedu a high-ranking noble girl receives before coming of age viewed with more prestige since friendships - and therefore connections - are more likely to be formed.

### Origin
This tradition started early in the Age of Heroes.
