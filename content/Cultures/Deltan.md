###### Summary
Blessed by the Twin Goddesses with a fertile landscape, and ruled over by the enigmatic god-king [[Deities#Sarru Zalalahara|Sarru Zalalahara]], Deltans live in a land of milk and honey where even the slaves never go hungry. Creatures born within The Delta are healthier, prettier, and more likely to be female.

Where other cultures have risen and fallen with the passage of time, Deltans have remained. Between their blessings and their timelessness, Deltans to consider themselves to be Civilized - and consider all other groups to be barbarians.

## Blessed by the Goddesses
The Temple of the Twin Goddesses is the state religion of Civilization and plays a major role in the lives of Deltan individuals. They praise [[Deities#Ianna, Goddess of Love and War|The Lioness]] and [[Deities#Nilvantra, Goddess of Life and Knowledge|Cultivation]] as two sides of the same coin, and revere the god-king [[Deities#Sarru Zalalahara|Sarru Zalalahara]] as their husband. The Priestesses that work there are divinely appointed and are considered equal to nobility. The Temple collectively passes laws, influences social norms, judges crimes, officiates weddings, and conducts research.

#### A Land of Beauty and Romance
Behavior that might be considered lustful or inappropriate in other cultures is often considered a method of worshiping [[Deities#Ianna, Goddess of Love and War|The Lioness]] and encouraged by the [[Temple of the Twin Goddesses]]. Due to the higher number of women, [[Deltan#Relationships between Women|relationships between women]] are common. The temple hosts an annual Fertility Festival and even offers romantic services in exchange for a donation. 

> [!info] Common Phrases Relating to The Lioness
> - “Blessings of the Lioness Upon You” - Used in a similar fashion to "Go Get a Room" or "Uwu"
> - “The goddesses seem to be arguing” - Phrase used when teenagers make an appearance mistake, which is practically a right of passage for Deltans.
> - "The Lioness Protects (from the cold)" - used in a similar manner to "Sluts don't get cold"

This worship can be done through dressing in an 'appealing' manner, with [Shenti (Skirt)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shendyt) commonly worn by both men and women, and everyone is obsessed with jewelry. When outside during the day, most don a light cloak of linen or cotton to keep the harsh sun off their backs. (for more, see [[Deltan#Appearance]]).

#### A Land of Life and Knowledge
Although not as fashionable as [[Deities#Ianna, Goddess of Love and War|The Lioness]], [[Deities#Nilvantra, Goddess of Life and Knowledge|Cultivation]] has a major presence in the lives of her people. Education is provided by the temple or authorized instructors, meaning most Deltans are literate. The Temple has a presence either directly or indirectly in most academic conferences and research worldwide. Cultivation's blessings have made more of the population female (about 60%) and more fertile. Disease is rare in the Delta, where priestesses keep watch over the health of the populace.

## An Authoritarian Society
Deltans have a strong class structure and Deltans (but not barbarians) are expected to follow it. [[Deltan#Nobility|Deltan Nobility]] have additional social and legal rights compared to other societies. The most powerful nobles belong to the Zalalahara Dynasty and other noble families that have been around for thousands of years.

#### Male Dominated
There are a series of social rules, laws, and other parts of Deltan society that reinforce male dominance. Women who are one of the [[Deltan#Serapis (Archmage)|Serapis (Archmage)]], a Priestess (ToTG), or women from 'barbarian' cultures get to ignore most of these rules - but the vast majority of women are bound by them. Other women choose to leave the Delta altogether in a practice known as the [[Balu]].

> [!info] Oppressive Laws and Practices
> - Deltan girls are expected to avoid direct insults, confrontation, and are generally expected to be subservient and submissive.
>- It is difficult legally for women to hold property or noble titles in their own right. Even when they do, they are frequently managed by a male relative.
>- Women aren’t allowed to fight on the frontlines, although ‘femme-fatale’ roles are much more acceptable (magicians, healers, rogues, ect.).
>- The vast majority of slaves are women, and the aesthetic is considered so pervasive that noble daughters are frequently 'technically' enslaved.
>- Due to the high number of women, it is common for women to marry downwards, such as the daughter of a noble marrying a merchant.

#### Slavery in the Delta
Slavery is prominent in The Delta. There are several classes of slaves, with the three most prominent classes of slavery being Sa (Slave Girl), Emedu, and Arad.

###### [[Sa (Slave Girl)]]
The vast majority of slaves belong to a subclass of Sa - while Sa literally means “slave”, few are male. Sa are typically treated decently and have legal protections. They typically wear a collar to symbolize their status.

###### [[Emedu]] (Handmaidens)
Noble daughters can be contracted as handmaidens until they come of age in exchange for being presented at a more prestigious court. Although technically slaves, the relationship is usually voluntary, and they typically wear a choker to symbolize their position.

###### Arad
For a few individuals who commit high crimes, they can expect to live a life of forced labor​ in the mines or fields. Most Arad are men. They are still well fed and have decent healthcare, in spite of otherwise dour conditions.

## The Test of Time
If a Deltan were to be transported back a thousand years, they would still be in a land where people spoke their language, dressed in the same clothes, and ate similar foods. A [[Sa (Slave Girl)#Sa-Geme|Sa-Geme]] would still serve them drinks at a bar, the Temple of the Twin Goddesses would still conduct the same religious ceremonies, and Sarru Zalalahara would still guide civilization from his eternal throne.

The biggest change would likely be what surrounds The Delta - the ever-shifting barbarians - and how Deltans relate to these barbarians. This isn’t a term used (entirely) as an insult - Deltan women admire the strength and barbarians, Stormrunner Hussars act as auxiliaries for units that protect civilization from raiders, and many Deltan merchants trade with their Falcotosian counterparts.

Even given that respect, many Deltans view barbarians paternalistically. Ultimately, the Temple of the Twin Goddesses has documented the beginnings, rise, changes, and fall of these cultures - and soon they’ll document the one of whichever barbarian is standing before them.

> [!question] Deltans are the only civilization that has truly stood the test of time, and typically view outsiders as barbarians. How does your character express this relationship?

#### Recent Events: Deltan Highstorm, The Queen of the Storm
There has been a more substantial shift in this psychology in the last two decades, however. In the 700th year of the Age of Heroes, a Highstorm went off track, travelling through The Delta and killing over two-thirds of its population.

Then, the Queen of the Storm, who had unified the Zephyral over the past decade, conquered Isin. When Sarru Zalalahara arrived to fight the Queen of the Storm, he refused to personally fight because of the Queen’s pregnancy - and the War of the Highstorm ended in an uneasy Zephyral victory. The Temple of Nephtys, a heresy of the Temple of the Twin Goddesses, was placed in Isin.

Some have viewed these events as a sign of Sarru Zalalahara losing his mandate to rule, or even the Temple of the Twin Goddesses as a divine authority. Others argue that within the Temples not a leaf was misplaced by the storm, and that the Queen of the Storm has given concessions since that date.

## Parties, Entertainment, and More
Parties in the Delta are extreme events, where people pride themselves on hedonism and embrace the glory of the Lioness. However, many of these events have strange rules that have socially developed over 3000 years.

#### Bars
Buying a round of drinks - and getting the party going - are especially well regarded activities in the Delta. Any bar worth its salt ideally has a cute [[Sa (Slave Girl)#Sa-Geme]] serving drinks to its customers.

> [!warning] Buying a Round
> Covering everyone’s drink at a bar is a mark of prestige to Deltans. Carousing (Downtime Option) provides two favors, instead of one. Buying drinks in a bar (about 50gp for a mid-tier bar) provides a +2 to Persuasion checks made against Deltans in that hour.

##### Bardic Pressure
To play an instrument is to be an aspect of Ianna, Goddess of Love and War - and to do so badly is practically heretical. A bad performance can lead to deep shame and social banishment. Onlookers may choose to graffiti the bar they are playing in, which the bard has to pay for. Heckling is common - with musicians sending people to heckle their rivals.

#### Party Basics
Most barbarians are aware that parties for the civilized are always a potluck - even if their host says its not. Beyond that, Deltans have a series of byzantine rules surrounding invitations.

##### Invitations
- The first person invited to a party has to plan and they aren’t always aware of this fact. For this reasons, guests of honor are invited after others are.
- If you somehow receive an invitation, you are invited to the party even if the invitation was not intended for you. (Does not count if stolen)

###### Kas'de'Imba
Principal that allows invitations to be socially declined under certain circumstances.
- The invitation must be cool/fancy, in proportion to the importance of the event.
- Nobles can also claim this right against non-nobility or lower ranked nobles.
> [!translation] Kas'De’Imba is Sumerian for Party + Decline.

#### Drugs
While technically illegal, drugs frequently appear at parties. Many drugs are seasonal however, leading to a large number of counterfeits, withdraws, and price gouging. This has lead the Temple of the Twin Goddesses to offer free testing for illicit substances.

#### Sports
Saquarra (Ancient contact volleyball), Archery (Massive yearly competition where the winner effectively becomes nobility), swimming, and rafting are popular national sports. There are frequently large sponsorships in exchange for advertising.

#### Festivals
Festivals require a certain number of people to be registered with the Temple of the Twin Goddesses, but after a basic vetting process are basically given free reign. One or more Priestesses are required to attend - Young Priestesses are frequently forced to be the attending priestess to learn what is and isn't against religious laws (and because no one wants to watch weird shit).

## Relationships between Women
Due to the large number of women in the Delta, relationships between women are surprisingly common across degrees of 'seriousness'.

#### Blue Feathers
Women who are entirely uninterested in men instead (voluntarily) wear a blue feather to signify their lack of interest. It is considered rude for women who do not genuinely feel this was to do so unless it is out of concern for safety. It should be noted that due to the lower status of women in the Delta, that many female couples struggle financially (this is purely based on sexism, not homophobia).

#### Kam
Some women will frequently engage in playful romances with each other for practice and companionship while they wait on a more permanent relationship to arrive. These relationships are understood to be transitory from the beginning, although even after they split the women often remain close friends with each other. (And sometimes, of course, they decide its more serious, or one does and there's a lot of fun drama).
> [!translation] ‘Kam’ is Sumerian for ‘To Desire’

## Appearance
Dressing in an appealing manner is considered essentially a form of worship among Deltans, with individuals often choosing to wear fewer clothes than their climate calls for (although, fortunately, the Delta is quite warm) - leading to the phrase “The Lioness protects from the cold”. For inspiration, look up some fantasy ancient Egyptian art.

Pants are uncommon among Deltans, with most(male or female) favoring skirts, such as the [Shenti (Skirt)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shendyt). Women may also wear dresses, but most working women instead wear a sports-bra like top or tops that connect to a collar. Richer women may choose to wear longer skirts, and tops that are a little less supportive. Men typically go topless, although may wear a vest.

> [!warning]
> Stepping on a woman’s skirt is considered a high faux pas to the point of being dueled or ostracized. It’s one of the reasons slave girls tend to wear shorter skirts.

When outside during the day, most Deltans don a light cloak of linen or cotton to keep the sun off their backs. Wealthier individuals may opt to instead be carried by a shaded palanquin or ride in a covered carriage. One could say the ability to dress minimally is a sign of wealth rather than a lack of it.

Jewelry is important to Deltan individuals, with many wearing multiple pieces of jewelry. Wealthier individuals frequently wear an [Usekh Collar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Usekh_collar). Many wear extensive makeup (such as [Kohl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kohl_\(cosmetics\))) and take care to remove most body hair.

Given the limited amount of clothes, Deltans frequently obsess over the trim of their cloaks, clothes, and skirts. Early in the [[Age of Heroes]] frilled trim was in fashion, although recently that has transitioned to a more sleek, modern trim.

## Nobility
Deltan Nobility have additional social and legal rights compared to other societies. The most powerful nobles belong to the Zalalahara Dynasty and other noble families that have been around for thousands of years.

### Noble Privileges
- Non-nobles cannot question the (non-sexual) affairs of nobles.
- Nobles can host other nobles when they are travelling (Hospitality Culture)
- Nobles can substitute favors instead of coin (Legally Enforceable)
- Nobles can invoke [[Deltan#Kas'De’Imba]] to decline invitations under certain circumstances

### Noble Ranks

#### Sarru (King, Hereditary)
Sarru are sovereign rulers, the equivalent of king or emperor. Among Deltan and related cultures, citizens do not have last names outside of those belonging to a royal dynasty. The titles are hereditary.

Sarru Zalalahara is the most prominent Sarru, though there are several other individuals who are Sarru and are part of (or claim to be part of) the Zalalahara Dynasty. Those individuals usually include their name in their title (for example, Sarru User'kera Zalalahara).

#### Lugal (High Lord, Hereditary)
Lugal are the lords of cities or regions. Sarru Zalalahara usually grants them in response to exemplary service to civilization - the titles are hereditary.

#### Sarrim/Sarrimisa (Relatives of a Sarru or a Lugal)
The naming conventions for relatives of a Lugal are similar to those of a Sarru. Sons of a Lugal are called Sarrim, and the Daughters and Wives of a Lugal are called Sarrimisa. Older naming conventions listed the wives and married daughters as Dumumi, but that practice is dated.

#### Ensi (High Lord, Appointed)
Ensi are the lords of cities or regions. Sarru Zalalahara either appoints them to the position as a reward for military service, or permits the citizens of the town to vote for their own. While the position is for life, it is not hereditary.

#### Hazzanu (Baron)
An appointed noble, typically ruling over a town and its surrounding villages. Often times used when the military controls a small, border town. Frequently reports directly to an Ensi or Lugal rather than Sarru Zalalahara. A Hazzanu can be appointed (for life) or hereditary.

##### Serapis (Archmage)
The state-sanctioned archmages of The Delta are equivalent to nobility in terms of rank, but not officially noble themselves. Unlike noble titles, there is no difference between male/female titles.

##### Vizer
A high ranking steward that takes the place of the reigning Lugal or Ensi while they are absent.

## Education
Most education is handled through Priestesses who educate everyone in major cities and visit smaller villages. They offer tutors one-on-one for the rich. Becoming a tutor otherwise is difficult, but not impossible. 

Other educators frequently act as a supplement to regular education. They are verified by the Temple of the Twin Goddesses and given a license for instruction - many of which were at one point [[Sa (Slave Girl)#Sa-Rig|Temple Slaves]]. Anyone who goes into instruction is guaranteed to be taken care of their entire life.

## Weddings
Virtually all weddings are conducted by a Priestess of the Temple of the Twin Goddesses. There’s many localized and individualized traditions, but the following are a couple common ones:
- Walking Into Life Together: It is considered a bad omen if either individual wears shoes during a wedding ceremony - feet have to be in contact with the earth or it may be considered a forced wedding by the Priestess (ToTG) conducting it. The couple are gifted a pair of sandles afterwards, frequently from their servants.
- A Maidenly Gift: If a man is from a substantially lower class than their partner, they are expected to provide some guarantee of well-being. This frequently comes in the form of a handmaiden.

#### Nin'geme
Individuals who are unable to afford a handmaiden may offer younger sisters or coin from selling their younger sisters instead. The sister can reasonably expect to be 'freed' if the families' financially situation improves.
> [!translation] Nin’geme is Sumerian for Sister+Maid
