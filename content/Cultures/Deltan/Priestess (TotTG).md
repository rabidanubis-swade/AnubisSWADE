Priestesses of the Temple of the Twin Goddesses always come from and are chosen in The Delta, typically by a sign from the goddesses; these signs can be varied and range from the altar lighting up to another priestess getting a vision. They enter training soon after they are selected, becoming a [[Priestess (TotTG)#Young Priestess|Young Priestess]]. Priestesses are exclusively female, with the exception of [[Nephtys]].

Temples, no matter how small, are operated by a pair of them. Usually one representing aspects of [[Deities#Ianna, Goddess of Love and War|Ianna]] and another representing aspects of [[Deities#Nilvantra, Goddess of Life and Knowledge|Nilvantra]].

#### Symbol
The head of a lioness with a 'mane' formed of vines.

# Rules and Privileges
- Priestesses are unable to have long-running romantic connections or marry outside of the temple. Any children the Priestesses have are given up to their fathers or an orphanage.
- Priestesses are considered ‘separate but equal’ to nobility in both the Delta and often times beyond it.

### Ranks and Honors
Priestesses can be given certain awards for going above and beyond simply representing the Lioness, but rather enacting their will.

- Archpriestess - Each goddess has one, typically spending half their time in Semara and the other half in holy sites across the Delta.
- High Priestess - Typically the head of a group of temples, such as those in a region or nation.
- Fang of the Lioness - The Highest Honor available to a priestess by representing the Lioness. Typically done for leadership in warfare, spreading love across the world (peace negotiations).
	- Cub of the Lioness - Young Priestess variant.
- Gardener of the Vine - The Highest Honor available to a priestess by representing Cultivation. Typically done for extensive academic achievement, educational ability, and healing.
	- Sapling of the Vine - Young Priestess variant.
- Recusant - A priestess who broke the rules surrounding the priestesshood in a severe way. The back of their hand is tattoo'd with a symbol of the temple of the twin goddess.

# Young Priestess
Young Priestesses can be selected by a sign of the goddesses as early as six, but typically in their pre-teens or early teens - less than three in a thousand girls in the Delta are selected. These Priestesses represent the future of the temple and spend their time in education, research, and training.

As soon as they are selected, the girls begin being conferred the rights and responsibilities of a Priestess. Chosen girls have about a month to arrange any necessary affairs before being taken to their place of training - for almost all young priestesses that is in Semara, though there are exceptions. Your relatives, friends, and even business associates will likely be celebrating this, as people consider their role in your life getting to this point.

> [!info] What if I was a Slave?
> No matter your social class, if you are a declared to be a priestess, you immediately become one. If a character was a [[Sa (Slave Girl)]] for example, and a sign was delivered, you would be freed. The [[Temple of the Twin Goddesses]] would offer your prior master recompense, but the master would likely refuse it or donate it back.

### Lifestyle
The life of a Young Priestess is a life of luxury - most individuals will find themselves surrounded by fine clothes, slaves and servants, entertainment, and other amenities unavailable to them beforehand.

Most dorms will have communal [[Sa (Slave Girl)]] assigned to them, although distinguished young priestesses may have been assigned their own servants. Either way, these servants are typically young so the priestesses learn loyalty, understanding, as well as practice being an authority figure.

> [!question] If making a character that is a Priestess, consider how they may react to this sudden rise in social standing if they came from poverty.

#### Dorm Assignment
Upon arriving in Semara, Young priestesses are typically separated into dorms based on their age and experience. Although calling them dorms is a bit of an understatement - each is an immaculate palace in their own right, designed to fit 200 to 500 girls. The dorm selection is barely understood either - though some dorms seem to have rumors about ending up with individuals of certain backgrounds or character traits. These dorms sort of act as their 'team' while they are there. A "Head Sister" - young priestesses that have recently graduated - keep watch over the day to day functioning of the dormitory and the girls within.

After they graduate priestesses tend to identify with the dorm they attended (or other institution if it was outside of Semara) the same way one might identify with a college after graduation.

### Training
Beyond the luxurious lifestyle however is a brutal training regimen; Young Priestesses are lectured in the main temple, participate in Socratic seminars, and expected to take a portion of their free time for constructive pursuits alongside their leisure. Virtually all priestesses are at least somewhat talented in the mystic arts - they are trained in their use of these along with the basics of martial arts.

### Younger Sisters
Young Priestesses start as Younger Sisters - Younger Sisters focus on learning the basics of religion, behavior, and knowledge necessary to be a successful Priestess. Due to the many backgrounds a priestess may come from, they frequently receive informal instruction from Older Sisters who act as mentors. While there is variation, most priestesses are considered younger sisters for about three years.
- Younger Sisters are usually required to be chaperoned when going beyond the temple, either by an older sister, guards, or a male relative.

### Older Sisters
About halfway through their training, Young Priestesses become Older Sisters. They can either remain at their current location or head off to a different posting to act as an assistant. Part of this decision may be based on need, but also may be dependent on the behavior of the Priestess, and the Priestesses desires. A committee of makes a decision: one of the priestesses' teachers, a caretaker (head sisters in Semara), a member of the temple leadership, a fellow younger priestess, and up to three individuals selected depending on circumstance and position vote on this. Many older sisters start a research thesis they seek to publish by the time they graduate.
